Sub-compositions and Amalgamations

Recognizing the compositional nature of sequencing data leads to several useful properties:

  • Sub-Compositions
    1. Ability to isolate sub-groups of probes from the sequenced gene set for focused analysis
    2. Can reduce noise from excluded genes with high variation
  • Amalgamations
    1. Can collapse groups of genes with the same functionality into a single proportion
    2. Facilitates the use of exploratory techniques for finding differences in pathways/functional groups
    3. Amalgamations retain compositional structure
