Properties of Compositional Data

  • Relative abundance data arise from a sum constraint: \(\sum_{i=1}^{D} t_i = \mathbb{T}\)
    • \(t_i\) gives the number of reads to sample \(i\) in a sequencing run with \(D\) samples.
    • The sum constraint, \(\mathbb{T}\), can change between platforms, sequencing runs, and samples.
    • This leads to an arbitrary difference in scale between samples.
  • Compositions lie in the \(D-1\) dimensional simplex (\(S^{D-1}\)). As a result of the constraints on compositional data, the covariance matrix of an un-transformed composition is difficult to interpret.
